Get Involved: Plant Trees in Your Community

If you’ve got more time than money, you can still be part of the reforestation solution. Here's how to get started planting trees the right way.

Best Practices for Tree Planting

  • Choose native species suited to your local climate and soil.
  • Check with local organizations to avoid planting invasives or duplicates.
  • Prepare the site: remove competing weeds, loosen the soil, and ensure access to water.
  • Plant during the rainy season (or fall/spring depending on region).
  • Water regularly for the first 1–2 years to ensure survival.

What You Can Do

  • Plant one tree a month in your yard or with a local group.
  • Organize community planting events.
  • Volunteer with regional forestry or conservation groups.

Every tree counts. You don’t need a fortune to grow a forest.
