Advance Market Commitments for Forest Restoration

Reforestation is one of the most powerful climate solutions available today. It’s fast, scalable, and cost-effective—but it’s chronically underfunded, especially at the early stages when capital is most needed.

The Challenge

  • High-quality reforestation credits are scarce: less than 3% of annual carbon credit demand is met by high-quality restoration projects on Verra.
  • Restoration projects face high upfront costs and long timelines—often 5+ years before revenue.
  • Traditional finance avoids these risks due to lack of collateral, long payback periods, and lower returns.

The Opportunity

Advance Market Commitments (AMCs) unlock stalled reforestation projects by committing capital now for future delivery of carbon credits. This derisks early-stage financing and sends a strong demand signal to the market.

How It Works

  • You commit today to purchase carbon credits from future reforestation projects at a fixed price.
  • We use your commitment to unlock low-cost loans and begin planting trees now.
  • You pay later—typically upon delivery of the verified credits (starting ~2028).

Example Deal Parameters

  • Commitment Size: $500,000 – $5,000,000
  • Credit Volume: 5,000 – 100,000 tCO₂
  • Price per Credit: $50–$100, depending on project type and term
  • Contract Term: 5–10 years (short) or 20+ years (long-term)
  • Delivery Start: ~2028
  • Project Types: Native high-biodiversity forests, agroforestry, blue carbon (mangroves)
  • Verification: Verra VM0047 + CCB Standards, 100% native species, community co-benefits

Climate and Financial Impact

  • Each credit represents 1 ton of CO₂ removed and stored long-term
  • Projects offer over 40 years of permanence
  • Early-stage credits can appreciate 3–10x in market value over time

Advance commitments de-risk early investment, unlock project finance, and accelerate the restoration of forests at scale. By stepping in at this critical phase, you make climate solutions possible today—not a decade from now.
